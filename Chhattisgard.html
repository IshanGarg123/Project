  1. Raipur

    2. Raipur is the capital city of the Indian state of Chhattisgarh. Raipur is a beautiful city situated in Chhattisgarh in Central India. Also, because it is the capital city of this state, Raipur is very significant and prominent, especially for its steel markets. Raipur is home to one of the biggest and largest steel markets in India and also one of the biggest industrial centers. It is also one of the largest cities of Chhattisgarh and hence visited by many tourists all over. Raipur has more than 200 steel mills and six steel plants. Apart from these, the city also boasts off aluminum, power and also coal industries. Raipur was initially a part of Madhya Pradesh before Chhattisgarh was formed and that happened in 2000 and since Raipur has made its place in the most visited places of the state.

    Thought the popular belief is that there is not much to see and do in this city, the fact remains that Raipur does have many tourist attractions apart from the steel industries and coal factories. From lakes to temples, there is a lot that one can see in Raipur. Plan a trip to this underexplored city of Raipur and see how it leaves a lasting impression on you. And for people who love shopping, Raipur is an absolute delight since Raipur is also famous for its terracotta. So while you are busy exploring Raipur, you can also indulge in some shopping. And if you happen to visit during festivals or events, then it is an added advantage, Raipur celebrates many festivals with much pomp and gaiety and to be a part of these festivals means to get an insight into the culture of this city.

    So now you know that Raipur is indeed a major tourist destination and apart from its industries has several other places of interest to see. And what make Raipur such a charming place to visit is also the many festivals and events that they host and celebrate with so much dedication. Raipur also has a short history attached to it about all its rulers and dynasties. Read to know what it is!

  2. Jagdalpur

    3. The beautiful city of Jagdalpur is located on the banks of river Indravati. The city is known as the paradise of tourists because it has housed a number tourist attraction including a number of waterfalls, beautiful lakes, archeological caves, temples filled with the fragrance of incense and spiritual music floating around the temple complex. Jagdalpur is also known as city of squares because it has 204 sqaures within the span of 8.75 sq km. Tourism is at its all time high during the famous "Bastar Dusherra". November to June is considered appropriate period to visit Bastar and Jagdalpur. Weather remains quite cool and one can also witness numerous festivals during these months in this part of Chhattisgarh. Further nature is at its full bloom which further adds to the excitement of the tourists.

    Jagdalpur is considered to be the paradise of tourists for its numerous sightseeing joints. The city offers every person the places of his/her choice. It has a number of tourists sights from temples to wildlife’s to waterfalls to lakes to rivers to museums and many more. Chitrakoot Waterfall is the broadest waterfall, considered to be the Niagara Falls of India because of its typical horse-shoed shape. Chitrakoot Falls are located at a distance of 38km from Jagdalpur only connected by road. This water body has height of 96 ft and the source of this waterfall is Indravati River.

    Kailash and Kutumar two natural caves are the second longest natural caves in the world, located 40km from Jagdalpur. They are located in the dense forests of Kanger Valley National Park near Tiratgarh Waterfalls which are main tourist attractions in Chattisgarh. The striking feature about these caves are the stalagmite and stalactites formation. These caves have many blind wells and consist of five chambers.

  3. Bhilai

    4. The city of Bhilai is located in the Durg district in Chhattisgarh. It is situated on National Highway No. 6,25 km away from Raipur, the capital city of Chhattisgarh. The largest steel plant of India, Bhilai Steel Plant is situated here. It is the only manufacturer of rails in the country. All major festivals like Diwali, Holi, Durga Puja, Id are celebrated with great vigour and pomp in the region. As the Sikh population in the region is high, a large number of Sikh festivals like Baisakhi, Guru Gobind Singh's Guruparba, etc. are celebrated here. Bhilai is also known as “Mini India” because of its multi religion population.

    The major tourist attractions in Bhilai are Maitri bagh, Civic Center, Kalibari, etc. Maitri Bagh is a zoo and a large number of visitors come here. The region is mostly an industrial area. There are many small temple complexes here.

    Ganga Maiya Temple is located at a distance of 60 Kms from the main town region of Bhilai, at Jhalmala. As per the mythical stories, it is so believed that a fisherman found the statue of the goddess in his fishing net; however he did not see the idol and thus it went ignored by him. Another dweller in the same village had a dream that he was being instructed for recovering the idol and then setting it up in a hut close to the village. Bhikam Chand Tiwari built a temple in its place at a much later time period. Maitri Bagh-Zoo is made into a park as well as a zoo. The Bhilai Steel Plant has established this structure adjacent to the two numbers of water bodies. The name Maitri Bagh stands for ‘friendship garden’. This place represents the accord and peace between two countries namely India and the USSR. The musical fountain and the shows related to it are the main attraction of this place. Numerous beautiful gardens, a sparkling lake, an artificial waterfall, a musical fountain and also an enjoyable toy train are some of the features of Maitri Bagh. Also, another structure named the Pragati Minar is present here, which offers a good view of the Maitri Bagh Park and also of the steel plant.

  4. Indravati National Park

    5. Indravati National Park is a national park located in Bijapur district of Chhattisgarh state in India. Indravati National Park is among the finest and most famous wildlife parks of Chhattisgarh. It is one of three Project Tiger sites in Chhattisgarh, along with Udanti-sitanadi, and is home to one of the last remaining populations of the endangered wild water buffalo. Indravati National Park has one of the last populations of the endangered wild Asian buffalo. The national park is also home to a variety of other ungulate species. The commonly found reptiles in the park are freshwater crocodile, monitor lizard, Indian chameleon, common krait, Indian rock python, cobra and Russell's viper to name a few. The park also gives shelter to the large variety of birds of which the hill myna is the most important species.

    The monsoons come in the month of July and temperatures during this season range from 22°C- 28°C, making it a tad cooler. This is a great time to visit the national park and check out the waterfalls in the region And also The best time to visit Indravati National Park is the winter time. Temperatures during this time range from 10°C-20°C, which you can imagine is quite cold. This is the perfect time to visit as the weather is apt for sightseeing and wildlife activity.

    Once you are done exploring the forest area of Indravati National Park, go ahead and enjoy a day with your family at the many waterfalls of the region. The most famous one near the national park is Malangir Waterfall in Dantewada. Spend a day at the banks of the waterfall to relax and have fun. When you visit a national park, it goes without saying that a safari is a must do. At Indravati National Park, you can either go on a jeep safari or you can opt to go on a boat safari as well. Experience the habitat of these wild creatures and birds and see them up close in their natural home.

    Shopping is an absolute must on any trip that you are taking and it’s no different when you visit national parks. Inside Indravati National Park, you can find several gift shops from which you can buy souvenirs of the national park, maps of the forest, key chains etc. But if you want a more authentic feel of shopping, you can head over to the nearby villages for a better experience. Villages have weekly markets known as haats where tribal men and women gather to sell handmade items like idols of gods and goddesses, metal items, hand woven cloth and a lot of jewellery that appeals to a lot of tourists. Shopping from such places supports the local tribes of the region which is something the state has always been stressing on.

  5. Barnawapara Wildlife Sanctuary

    6. Barnawapara Wildlife sanctuary, is named after Bar and nawapara forest villages, which are in the heart of the sanctuary. It is a land mass of undulating terrain dotted with numerous low and high hillocks well forested area of North-eastern corner of Raipur district. The Tributaries of Mahanadi are the source of water. River Balam Delhi forms the western boundary and Jonk River forms the north-eastern boundary of the Sanctuary. The well stocked forests of the Sanctuary Classified as Teas sal and mixed forests. This sanctuary is famous for the frequent sighting of the Indian bison (Gaur) Cheetal, Sambhar, Neelgai, Wild boar are commonly seen.Bar-Nawapara boosts of over 150 species Birds.

    The best time to visit Barnawapara Wildlife Sanctuary is during the winters that last from last week of October to March. Temperatures in this season range from 5°C-12°C in the peak months of the season. The climate is pleasant and you are able to see more wildlife activity. On your trip to Barnawapara Wildlife Sanctuary, you must not lose out on spending some time with the local villagers and hear their stories about the forest. The best way to stay in a home stay for a couple of days and enjoy the food and company of the tribal villagers.

    While you are in Barnawapara Wildlife Sanctuary, don’t forget to go on a jeep safari inside the forest to get a full feel of the sanctuary. You can go on a safari by jeep or you can go on a bicycle along with a guide to see the animals and birds inside. Once you are inside the sanctuary, visit the watering holes in the region to see animals coming from all over the forest to drink water and bathe. This is a wonderful site to see and is enjoyed by anyone visiting the sanctuary.

    In Barnawapara Wildlife Sanctuary, you will get meals at the resort itself and will also have room service. But if you want to explore the local cuisine of the region, then you must visit the villages nearby. While you are doing a home stay, the villagers will feed you some of the tastiest food you have ever eaten. It is made with some of the rawest spices gotten from the very land and fresh vegetables. Most of the cuisine is non vegetarian in nature, but you will also find quite a few vegetarian dishes. If you want to eat at a restaurant, there are several joints in the region; you can try Kodar Restaurant on National Highway 53, Dhilan Fouji Dhaba, Yadav Bhog Bhandar and many more local joints that you will find in the area.

    The most authentic type of shopping can be down from the tribal markets that are set up every morning and evening; this is the primary livelihood of the villagers. You can find various items like cotton fabrics, wooden object that includes furniture, terracotta figurines, bell metal items, stone statues and so much more to choose from. You can also get idols of gods and goddesses right outside the several temples that are strewn all over the region. There aren’t any big shopping complexes, but rather these local markets are the most popular ones.
